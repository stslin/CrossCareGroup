Common Questions

Complex Care FAQs

Who are Complex Care programs designed for?
They are designed for clients whose needs are more complex than standard community support—such as people leaving hospital who require structured post‑discharge support, hospital‑substitute care at home under protocols, or in‑home rehabilitation—where it is clinically appropriate and safe to deliver care in the home.
What should we have ready if we’re exploring a Complex Care pathway?
It helps to have the discharge summary, current medication list, key contacts (hospital team, GP, family, coordinator), a sense of the person’s goals, and information about the home environment. We’ll then confirm which program—if any—is suitable and explain the next step. If we’re not the right service for this need, we’ll help you find the right pathway.
Are these programs available now?
Complex Care programs are being built and scaled using a safety-first gate model. Availability may vary by location and readiness. If you tell us what you’re trying to organise, we’ll confirm the most appropriate next step.
How do you keep Complex Care programs safe?
Safety comes before speed. Programs must pass gated checks on scope, inclusion/exclusion, competence, documentation, indicators and privacy/security before operating, and are then independently audited before being accepted to scale. Acceptance tests cover safety, clinical effectiveness, operational reliability, digital/records integrity and client experience.
How do you manage escalation?
We use clinically governed escalation pathways and clear communication. If a situation is urgent or life-threatening, always call 000.
