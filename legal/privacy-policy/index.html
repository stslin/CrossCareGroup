Privacy Commitment

Handling your clinical data with the highest standards of enterprise integrity and transparency.

Last updated: February 2026

1. About This Policy

[ENTITY LEGAL NAME] (ABN: [ABN NUMBER]), trading as Cross Care Group ("we", "us", "our"), is committed to protecting the privacy of your personal information. Our registered address is [REGISTERED ADDRESS].

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose and protect personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) where applicable.

By using our website or submitting information through our forms, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to provide our services. The types of information we collect depend on your interaction with us:

2.1 Support Requests

Full name, phone number, email address, support type, description of support needs, and consent status.

2.2 Contact Enquiries

Full name, email address, phone number, reason for enquiry, message content, catalogue preference, and consent status.

2.3 Referrals (including via JotForm)

This is our most data-intensive collection point and may include:

  • Referrer details: Name, organisation, phone, email, relationship to client
  • Client details: Full name, date of birth, address, phone, email
  • Emergency contacts: Name, relationship, phone, email
  • Funding information: NDIS plan dates, goals, plan management type, plan manager details, insurer details, claim numbers, funding amounts
  • Health and sensitive information: Primary and secondary diagnoses, risk flags (falls, skin integrity, seizures, behavioural concerns), clinical snapshots, current supports, medication details, hospital discharge information
  • Service details: Requested services, urgency level, delivery method preferences
  • Attachments: Uploaded documents (PDF, DOC, DOCX, JPG, JPEG, PNG up to 10MB per file)
  • Consent and authority: Consent to collect and store data, authority to act on behalf of client

2.4 Expressions of Interest (Careers)

Full name, email, phone, preferred location, desired role, availability, professional registrations, CV/resume upload, narrative statement, and consent status.

2.5 Feedback and Complaints

Name (optional), contact details (optional for feedback, required for complaints), message content, desired outcomes, and consent to be contacted.

2.6 Technical Information

When you visit our website, the following technical information may be collected automatically:

  • IP address (transmitted to Google via Google Fonts loading)
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and time spent on the site
  • Referring website

3. Sensitive Information

Under the Privacy Act 1988 and APP 3, "sensitive information" includes health information, disability status, and other categories that require additional protections. Under the GDPR, this is referred to as "special category data" (Article 9).

We collect sensitive information (including health and disability information) through our referral forms. This information is collected:

  • With your explicit consent (or the consent of your authorised representative)
  • Where it is directly related to and reasonably necessary for providing our care services
  • In accordance with APP 3.3 and GDPR Article 9(2)(a) and (h)

4. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To assess, plan and deliver care services tailored to your needs
  • To process and respond to support requests and enquiries
  • To process referrals and coordinate care with your existing providers
  • To evaluate expressions of interest for employment
  • To manage and respond to feedback and complaints
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations (including NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission requirements)
  • To improve the quality and safety of our services
  • To communicate with you about your care or enquiry

We do not use your personal information for direct marketing purposes unless you have explicitly opted in (e.g., requesting our service catalogue).

5. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

Where the GDPR applies, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): Where you have given clear consent for us to process your data for a specific purpose (e.g., form submissions).
  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Where processing is necessary to perform a contract with you or take pre-contractual steps at your request (e.g., service agreements).
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): Where processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation (e.g., NDIS reporting).
  • Vital interests (Article 6(1)(d)): In emergency situations where processing is necessary to protect your life or health.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): Where processing is necessary for our legitimate interests (e.g., improving service quality), provided those interests do not override your fundamental rights.

For special category data (health information), we rely on explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a)) and/or the health or social care exemption (Article 9(2)(h)).

6. Third-Party Data Processors and Disclosure

We share personal information with the following categories of third-party service providers who assist us in delivering our services:

Provider Purpose Data Shared Location
Microsoft (Power Automate) Form submission processing and workflow automation All data submitted through website forms (support, contact, referral, EOI, feedback) Australia / United States
JotForm Inc. Referral form hosting (Community Care and Allied Health) Referral form data entered within JotForm-hosted forms United States
Google LLC (Google Fonts) Typography and font delivery IP address, browser metadata United States

SiteGround (hosting) Website hosting and delivery Server access logs (IP addresses, request data) Australia

We may also disclose personal information to:

  • Your care team members (doctors, occupational therapists, registered nurses, allied health professionals) as part of your integrated care plan
  • NDIS plan managers, support coordinators, and the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission
  • Insurers and case managers (where relevant to your funding arrangement)
  • Government authorities where required by law

We never sell or disclose your personal information for marketing purposes to any third party.

7. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Some of our third-party service providers are located outside of Australia, primarily in the United States. Under APP 8, before disclosing personal information to an overseas recipient, we take reasonable steps to ensure the recipient handles the information in accordance with the APPs.

For individuals in the European Economic Area (EEA), cross-border transfers to countries outside the EEA are conducted in compliance with GDPR Article 46, relying on:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
  • The EU-US Data Privacy Framework (where applicable)
  • Your explicit consent where no other safeguard is available

8. Data Security

Cross Care Group is a Microsoft-first organisation. We take the security of your personal information seriously and implement the following safeguards:

  • HTTPS encryption (HSTS enforced) for all data in transit
  • Content Security Policy (CSP) headers to prevent cross-site scripting
  • Client-side input sanitisation and file validation
  • Rate limiting on form submissions
  • CORS origin allowlisting
  • Healthcare-standard encrypted cloud environments for data storage
  • Multi-factor authentication and audit trails for internal access

While we take all reasonable steps to protect your information, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to promptly addressing any suspected breaches in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act 1988 and GDPR Article 33.

9. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by law. Our standard retention periods are:

Data Category Retention Period Basis
Referrals and health/clinical data 7 years after last service Healthcare record-keeping obligations
Support requests and contact enquiries 3 years from date of enquiry Service delivery and quality assurance
Feedback and complaints 2 years from resolution Regulatory compliance and quality improvement
Expressions of interest (careers) 1 year from submission Recruitment purposes
Website server logs 90 days Security monitoring

When personal information is no longer required, it is securely destroyed or de-identified in accordance with APP 11.2 and GDPR Article 17.

10. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses the following technologies that may store or access information on your device:

  • Essential cookies: Required for basic website functionality (e.g., cookie consent preferences). These do not require consent.
  • Google Fonts: Loaded from Google servers, which transmits your IP address to Google LLC. This is used to display website typography.
  • YouTube embeds: Where we embed YouTube videos, YouTube may set cookies on your device when videos are played.
  • Google Analytics: If enabled, we may use Google Analytics (GA4) to understand how visitors use our website. This is subject to your consent via our cookie banner.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time using the cookie settings accessible via the banner at the bottom of each page, or through your browser settings.

11. Your Rights

11.1 Under the Australian Privacy Principles

Under the APPs, you have the right to:

  • Access (APP 12): Request access to personal information we hold about you
  • Correction (APP 13): Request correction of personal information that is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant or misleading
  • Complaint (APP 1): Lodge a complaint about our handling of your personal information

11.2 Under the GDPR (where applicable)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the GDPR otherwise applies, you also have the right to:

  • Erasure (Article 17): Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restriction (Article 18): Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • Data portability (Article 20): Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
  • Object (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent (Article 7(3)): Withdraw your consent at any time where consent is the basis of processing
  • Automated decision-making (Article 22): Not be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing

11.3 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of your rights, you may:

  • Email our Privacy Officer at: [PRIVACY_OFFICER_EMAIL]
  • Call us on: 1300 591 861 (Mon-Fri, 8:30am - 5:30pm AEST)
  • Write to: Privacy Officer, [REGISTERED ADDRESS]

We will respond to access and correction requests within 30 days. If we refuse a request, we will provide written reasons and information about how you can complain about the refusal.

12. Complaints

If you believe we have breached your privacy, you may lodge a complaint by contacting our Privacy Officer using the details above. We will acknowledge your complaint within 7 days and aim to resolve it within 30 days.

If you are unsatisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with:

  • Australia: The Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au or by calling 1300 363 992
  • NDIS participants: The NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission at www.ndiscommission.gov.au or by calling 1800 035 544
  • EU/EEA residents: Your local data protection supervisory authority. A list is available at edpb.europa.eu

13. Children's Privacy

Where we collect personal information about children (under 18), we do so with the consent of a parent, guardian, or authorised representative. This commonly occurs through referral forms submitted by health professionals or family members on behalf of a child receiving care.

14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Material changes will be communicated via a notice on our website. The "Last updated" date at the top of this policy indicates when it was most recently revised.
